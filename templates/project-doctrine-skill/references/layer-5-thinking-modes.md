# L5 — Thinking Modes

> **Real-time reasoning postures.** Not methods, not SOPs — how to notice how you're thinking and adjust while working.

These are stable. Most projects have 3–7 thinking modes worth naming. More than 10 and they've started to overlap.

## Reference examples

> **5.1 — Observe your own drift**
>
> **What it is:** A continuous metacognitive check on your own output-in-progress.
>
> **When to enter it:** Always on. Especially during long sessions or when a task is stalling.
>
> **How to hold it:**
> - Notice if you're listing when you should be judging
> - Notice if you're adding rules when you should be understanding the failure
> - Notice if you're defending a decision when you should be examining it
>
> **How to notice you've dropped it:** Your paragraphs get longer, hedging increases, you start using "comprehensive" and "thorough" as adjectives.

> **5.2 — Surface uncertainty explicitly**
>
> **What it is:** When you don't know the answer, say so before continuing.
>
> **When to enter it:** Any moment you catch yourself about to produce confident-sounding output on shaky evidence.
>
> **How to hold it:**
> - Pause and name what specifically is uncertain
> - State what evidence you have vs. what you'd need
> - Propose a cheap check before committing
>
> **How to notice you've dropped it:** Fluent-sounding output that, if pressed, you couldn't back up.

> **5.3 — Hunt counter-examples before confirming**
>
> **What it is:** Before accepting a thesis as correct, spend 30 seconds looking for what would invalidate it.
>
> **When to enter it:** Anytime you've generated a hypothesis and are about to act on it.
>
> **How to hold it:**
> - Ask: "what would have to be true for this to be wrong?"
> - Look for the nearest counter-case, not the most distant
> - Weight disconfirming evidence higher than confirming
>
> **How to notice you've dropped it:** Your explanation keeps finding reasons why the thesis is right; no real attempt to break it.

## Anti-patterns

- **Thinking modes that are actually methods.** If it has concrete steps and a defined output, it's L3 or L4.
- **Platitudes.** "Think carefully" is not a thinking mode — it has no trigger and no test.
- **Modes without failure signals.** A mode you can't notice yourself dropping is a mode you won't maintain.
- **Too many modes.** If you have 15, agents won't remember to apply any. Consolidate.

## Maintenance

- L5 entries are rarely added. When one is, it's usually because a systematic reasoning failure was noticed across multiple sessions.
- Retirement is even rarer. A dropped thinking mode is usually replaced, not simply removed.
